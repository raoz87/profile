Simplesurance

schutzklick-makler is a digital broker app to manage users insurance contracts and claims.

Role: Product Designer

As the only product designer for the product schutzklick-makler, I worked extensively to improve the app and roll out document/photo upload feature so that the user can upload their insurance documents and images easily on the app. I also worked on changing the authentication process from mobile number to email and password.

I did the following tasks during my tenure:

  1. Conduct Audits to identify usability issues with the product
  2. Conduct user research
  3. Conduct interview with internal stakeholders
  4. Bring design unity between Simplesurance products
  5. Work with the content manager to design campaigns
  6. Reiterate on existing information architecture and improve findability
  7. Develop low and high fidelity wireframes and prototypes
  8. Documentation

UX and Content audits

The purpose of conducting audit was to assess the user experience of the product in its current state and identify areas of improvement.

Information Architecture

I created the information architecture to understand how the pages were organised in the current format and linked the pages to the audit report.

User Journey

To visualise the steps and the users journey to complete important tasks, I created the journey map. The one shown here is the journey of installing and registering to use the app

With the journey map, I was able to highlight the long process of completing the sign-up on the app and opportunities to improve process and experience for the user.

Upload Document

The ‘upload document with camera functionality’ was a business requirement. The following were my UX deliverables for the feature:

  • Stakeholder interviews
  • Service blueprint
  • Gather technical constraints
  • Lo-fi wireframes
  • hi-fi wireframes
  • Prototypes

Internal Interviews

The purpose of conducting the internal interviews was to understand the current processes to create a service blueprint for new features (Document/Photo Upload & Authentication via Login and Password).

Service Blueprint and Userflows

With knowledge of internal processes in hand, I worked on creating service blueprint in order to visualize the relationships between different service components — people, props (physical or digital evidence), and processes — that are directly tied to touchpoints in a specific customer journey.

I created user flows to identify entry points to introduce the feature to the user as well as to identify the steps the user will need to take in order to use the feature.
