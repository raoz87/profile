Inktank

Inktank is a webresponsive app that is one place to find tattoos, studios and artists

The problem: To create an app that helps users find tattoo inspirations. As many as 1 in 5 people regret some aspect of getting a tattoo. How to help people make decisions that they won’t regret. Help match people with a tattoo artist that suits their style, personality and improve the whole tattoo experience.

Competitor Analysis

I chose 2 leading companies in the tattoo app industry, Tattoodo & Inkhunter. The analysis included key objectives, overall strategy, market advantage, marketing prole, SWOT, UX analysis – usability, layout and navigation structure.

User Interviews & Analysis

I conducted interviews as I wanted to understand the user's behaviour and attitudes towards getting a tattoo. I interviewed 5 people via phone and face to face from the target age group 18-49. I used an affinity mapping technique to organise and map the data and to gather insights.

Insights

  • All users would like to find artists by location and read reliable reviews.
  • Most users do surf the internet for information, some even refer to books for designs.
  • Users trust the friend circle to get a recommendation and trust it more than the internet reviews.

    User Interviews & Analysis

    I conducted interviews as I wanted to understand the user's behaviour and attitudes towards getting a tattoo. I interviewed 5 people via phone and face to face from the target age group 18-49. I used an affinity mapping technique to organise and map the data and to gather insights.

    Card sorting & Sitemap

    To organise data well, I created sitemaps with co-existing hierarchical pattern and refined the sitemap after conducting a card sorting session (with optimal sort).

    Insights

    • ‘FAQ’ was split 50-50 between having it in home page or under Guide page
    • Few of the participants put the ‘How to choose an artist?’,’How to choose a tattoo?’,’Tattoo Basics’ and ‘Risk & aftercare’ were placed under tattoos and Artists page. This suggests that users would like to get guidance while they are in the respective pages.
    • 2 out of the 6 participants would like the ‘log out’ in the home page rather than in the profile page.

    Usability Testing

    Issues found

    • Testers had difficulty finding(missed) virtually tried tattoo’s tab
    • Inktank home button is confusing/not clear
    • Confused favourites tab with add favourites
    • Did not understand what box next to search box is for
    • I don’t know what this icon is (sort)

    A/B testing

    I wanted to identify which type of favourite button the user liked to use. I tested screen A with just the heart icon and screen B with save and icon.

    Users chose Option 1 as the majority with 63.64% and some of the reasons why the user’s selected Option 1 because it is the simpler solution and heart icon itself says enough and that heart icon is self-explanatory, etc.
